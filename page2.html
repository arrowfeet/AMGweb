AMG History

Two men with a passion for motorsport and engineering founded AMG.
They saw an opportunity on every wide open road, an invitation in every curve, an affirmation in every victory: for more performance and more power.

Start & History of AMG

   AMG was founded and started by two incredibily intellegent former Mercedes-Benz engineer Erhard Melcher and Hans Werner Aufrecht at 1967. At first, Aufrecht was a huge Motorsport fan who worked at the Mercedes engine department, specialized in making the engine perform higher than making and developing the engine of the vehicle. However due to the worst result that Mercedes got back from the "Le Mans 24" Mercedes did not want to produce or work on high-performance vehicles or Motorsport. Aufrecht and Melcher decide to leave the company because he knew that he won't be able to do what he is specialized at it.


   They opened a shop at Burgstall, Germany to test, design, Produce racing engines. They soon discovered that Mercedes engines had a demand for high-performance upgrades and customization. They started to produce high-quality performance vehicles which were proved by returning with multiple wins from multiple diffrent races. By the end of 1999, Mercedes went all-in with AMG, buying a controlling share of AMG and making AMG cars part of the official Mercedes-Benz line-up after recognizing the passion and effort.

AMG Performace

   It has been more than 50years since the start of AMG. Numerous AMG engines and final products are considered revolutionary. One of the most revolutionary products that Mercedes introduced to the world was the AMG Hammer Sedan. It was a modified version of Mercedes E-class(W124) which proved AMG's performance modification to a new level by squeezing 5.6-liter V8 with quad-valve technology into a mid-size sedan. It was the world's fastest passenger Sedan at the time.


Out of multiple incredible achievements that AMG made, the recent legendary achievements that AMG made was Mercedes-Benz SL 65 AMG Black Series which was launched in the year 2008. It had a 6.0-liter turbocharged V12 producing 661hp. SL 65 featured pure-bed motorsport technology along with lightweight structure and design introducing sports car fans to a whole new world in terms of dynamic performance.
